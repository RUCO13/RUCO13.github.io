Optical properties of 2D GeTe under strain: A DFT study

Latest Publication

Abstract Summary

We used computer simulations to study how stretching or compressing a super-thin layer of germanium telluride (GeTe) changes its electronic properties. When we stretch the material by just 2%, it switches from one type of semiconductor to another, and its energy gap drops from 1.80 to 1.62 eV. This work shows how mechanical strain can be used to tune material properties for better devices.

Key Results

  • 2% strain triggers major electronic changes
  • Bandgap tuning from 1.80 to 1.62 eV
  • Optical properties become direction-dependent
  • Deformation potential measured at -6.45 eV

Why It Matters

This is my first publication using quantum mechanical calculations (DFT) to predict material behavior. The results show we can "tune" GeTe like adjusting a radio - just by stretching or compressing it, we can control its electronic properties for specific applications.

Applications: Flexible electronics, optical devices, solar cells, sensors
